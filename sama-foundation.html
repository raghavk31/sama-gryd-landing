The Problem

India's prosumers are generating clean energy into a market that cannot see them.

01
Forecasting is impossible

Rooftop solar generation varies by cloud, season, and monsoon. No existing tool gives a prosumer 24-hour ahead visibility into what they will generate, or what that will be worth.

02
The market pays nothing

Surplus exports at ₹2.50–4/unit — DISCOM's feed-in tariff. The market rate for the same unit is ₹6.80. The prosumer has no mechanism to access the difference.

03
DISCOMs have no visibility either

Distribution companies manage a grid increasingly full of two-way flows from rooftop solar. Their forecasting tools were built for one-way centralised generation.

04
The software layer does not exist

India Energy Stack launched. KERC P2P regulations are live. 250 million smart meters are deploying. The hardware is arriving. The intelligence layer has not been built.

What We're Building

Three layers. One platform.

01
Read

Connect every prosumer inverter. Read production, consumption, and surplus in real time. The first unified view of distributed generation — consumer-visible and consumer-owned.

Growatt · Solis · Huawei · Enphase
02
Match

A P2P energy marketplace. Prosumers sell surplus to verified buyers at market rate — ₹6.80, not ₹2.50. Settled via Beckn Protocol and UPI. India's first consumer energy market.

₹2.50 → ₹6.80 per unit
03
Aggregate

Pool prosumers into virtual power plants. Dispatch at grid scale. The consumer wedge becomes utility infrastructure — Sama becomes the coordination layer between distributed generation and the grid operator.

Consumer wedge → grid infrastructure
Why Now

The infrastructure is being built. We are building the software on top of it.

Four signals converged in 2024–2026 to make this the right moment. Each one alone is interesting. Together they are decisive.

Regulation
KERC P2P regulations live since August 2024

Karnataka domestic prosumers can sell directly to neighbours. DISCOMs must empanel approved service providers. The legal basis for Sama in Bengaluru.

Infrastructure

UPI-style open protocol for energy, led by Nandan Nilekani. Beckn handles matching, UPI handles settlement. The coordination layer now exists.
