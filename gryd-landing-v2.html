The Problem

Rooftop solar in India is a one-way street.

1.4 million Indian rooftops generate solar power today. None of them choose where it goes, when it's sold, or what it's worth. The DISCOM is the only buyer.

Visibility

Production data lives inside your inverter's app, in kilowatts and timestamps.

Indecipherable to most owners.
Choice

Surplus exports at the DISCOM's feed-in tariff. ₹2.50–4 per unit.

No alternative. No negotiation.
Agency

Even with smart meters arriving nationwide, there is no consumer-facing market.

Until now.
Why Now

India's energy stack is being rewired in public.

The Thesis

The next utility company in India will not look like a utility company.

Energy is the last consumer vertical in India that hasn't had its Robinhood moment. Not because the regulation isn't there — it is. Not because the infrastructure isn't ready — it's being built. What's missing is a consumer product that makes the invisible visible: your rooftop as an asset, your surplus as a position, your building as a micro-grid.

We are starting with the simplest wedge: show you what you're losing. Build trust through transparency. Then, when the trades begin, be the marketplace that was already there. From ₹2.50 to ₹6.80 is not a product feature. It's a category shift — from passive consumer to active market participant.
